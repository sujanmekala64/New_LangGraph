*2 April 2011 – 30 March 2011, 137 matches, 27 teams, 125 000 + fans.

## 1. Introduction & Context

The 2011 Cricket World Cup was more than just a four‑month festival of fast‑balls and centuries; it was an emotional crescendo that brought together a continent and a world that had long been obsessed with 80‑over cricket.

| **Key Factor** | **Why It Matters** |
|----------------|---------------------|
| **Hosts:** India, Sri Lanka & Bangladesh | *First ever co‑host arrangement, literally—this handed the ICC a “triple‑host” stage that echoed Mumbai’s fan fervour, Sri Lanka’s coastal calm and Bangladesh’s passionate crowds.* |
| **Teams:** 27 | *A record at the time, with legends, minnows and “one‑character” teams all packed into the same trophy—surges of unpredictability across a binary list of favourites.* |
| **Matches:** 137 | *The longest World‑Cup cycle ever, giving fans enough drama to keep the “World Cup” jargon alive daily.* |
| **TV Reach & Attendance** | *Globally streamed to 147‑plus countries and drawing a combined 1.85 billion pay‑TV viewers. In Mumbai’s Wankhede alone, over 90,000 fans filled the stadium.* |

The tournament carried a pre‑season buzz that echoed the “Jai Jawan” hype of 2007 but with a twist: *India’s chances had shifted from mere hope to probability.* The Indian gang, wrapped in son‑in‑law and ex‑captain “Sage” moments,’d sat in the long‑term “levels” segment for 28 years since 1983.

## 2. Organizational Triumphs (and Hiccups)

### Logistical Marvels

*Six venues* were selected—four in India, one in Sri Lanka and one in Bangladesh. They were more than pitches; they were the stage for the culture that would characterize the event.

The *infrastructure overhaul* saw new stands, revamped drainage systems in Kandy resisting monsoon waters, state‑of‑the‑art athlete amenities, and pre‑match civic events (Lavender‑Themed Lobbies, bonfire concerts).  

### Cultural Highlights

The opening ceremony at Wankhede featured a dance number that rumbled the stadium shell—a colorful confluence of Indian classical, Bollywood jazz and English folk. The official anthem, **“Sarkar”**, written and performed by Shankar‑Ehsan’s haunting voice, captured the triumvirate spirit: “Bharat‑Sri‑Bangla—duniya se khush karke pyara chumne chatu hai!”  

A quick fact—**a cumulative 5.2 million spectators** came across the three countries’ borders, enjoying 139 recorded curated digital feeds.  

### The Hiccup

Late‑night Part‑T20’s were slotted earlier than planned, resulting in a 30‑minute delay at the start of the Bangladesh–Kenya match. Security clouds of fans, heavy rain in Kandy and UK try‑out confusion also tested the managers. But overall, the pannings were celebrated as a *“glorious learning curve”* for ICC.

> **MS Dhoni (captain of India):** “The fans were glorious, the pitches were forgiving. Everyone lifted that trophy; we did a great job for the “world.”

## 6. Legacy & Impact on Global Cricket

### Birth of “Match‑of‑The‑Year” Award

With 27 teams, 113 capped captains, wide bowling coverage and unique all‑rounders, there was a truly 2011 cricket conversation about *What defined the tournament?*

**“Match‑of‑The‑Year”** started as an ad‑hoc celebration but quickly cemented itself into the Classics Calendar; the “Maheshcard” label was proudly adopted by ESPN.

### India’s Cricket Economy

*After the 2011 victory,* the Indian football trade skyrocketed, badminton & hockey wages doubled, and new corporate sponsors were proofed. Within a year, the country produced a *cricket-friendly social media club* that powered a new generation of talent.

### ICC Alterations

The success of 2011 helped launch the **World T20** in 2012, the new major calendar addition. The match‑population numbers forced a lot of workload management.

### Controversies

**2015 ICC Suspension** was because *“2010–2011 poll”*—the list of BCCI official overs required a far‑post, which the ICC deemed “political interference”. Through that crisis, new guidelines were written to ensure the *touch‑and‑go* between the International and local boards.
